The fox and the grape
One hot summer’s day a fox was strolling through an orchard till
he came to a bunch of Grapes just ripening on a vine which has been
trained over a lofty branch.
“Just the thing to quench my thirst,” quote he.
Drawing back a few paces, he took a run and a jump, and just missed the bunch.
Turning round again with a One, Two, Three, he jumped up, but with no greater success.
Again and again he tried, but at last had to give up,
and walked away with his nose in the air,
saying “I am sure they are sour.”

see also = ทำให้หายอยาก

Again = อีกครั้ง

Moral of the story

“A bad workman always blames his tools.”
